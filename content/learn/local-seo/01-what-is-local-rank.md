The **local rank** number on your dashboard is your average position in Google's
local "map pack" — the little map with three business listings that shows up for
searches like _"roofer near me"_ or _"emergency plumber [your city]"_.

## Why it's an average

Local rankings aren't a single number. Google shows different results depending
on **where the searcher is standing**. So we measure your position across a grid
of points around your service area and average them. That's why your dashboard
also has a **heatmap** — it shows the grid, green where you rank well and red
where you don't.

## Why it moves

Local rank shifts for reasons both in and out of your control:

- **Reviews** — volume, recency, and rating all feed the algorithm.
- **Proximity** — you'll always rank stronger nearer your physical location.
- **Your Google Business Profile** — categories, services, posts, and photos.
- **Competitors** — if a competitor earns reviews faster, you can slip even
  while doing everything right.

## What moves the needle most

Consistently earning reviews and keeping your Google Business Profile complete
and active are the two highest-leverage things you can do. The
["Respond to a Google review"](/learn/local-seo/02-respond-to-a-review) lesson
walks through the review side.
