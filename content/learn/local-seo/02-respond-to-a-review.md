Responding to reviews signals to Google that your business is active, and it
shows future customers you care. Aim to reply to **every** review within a few
days.

## Replying to a positive review

Keep it short, specific, and human:

1. Thank them by name.
2. Reference something specific they mentioned (the job, the tech, the timing).
3. Invite them back or mention a related service.

> _"Thanks so much, Maria! We're glad the team got your AC back up before the
> weekend. Don't hesitate to call us for that furnace tune-up this fall."_

## Replying to a negative review

Stay calm and professional — you're really writing for **everyone else** who
will read it later:

1. Acknowledge their experience without getting defensive.
2. Apologize for the frustration (not necessarily fault).
3. Move the details offline: give a name and a phone number.

> _"I'm sorry the scheduling didn't go smoothly, John. That's not the standard
> we hold ourselves to. Please call me directly at (555) 123-4567 — I'd like to
> make it right. — Dave, Owner"_

## What not to do

- Don't argue specifics publicly.
- Don't share customer private details.
- Don't use the exact same canned reply on every review — Google and readers
  both notice.

Steady, genuine replies compound over time and feed directly into your
[local rank](/learn/local-seo/01-what-is-local-rank).
